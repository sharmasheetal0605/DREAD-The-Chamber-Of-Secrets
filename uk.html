Pluckley Village, Kent



Nestled amidst peaceful countryside, in a quiet part of Kent you’ll find the picturesque village of Pluckley,
famously used as the backdrop in the 1990s for shooting of The Darling Buds of May.
Despite appearances, Pluckley is widely considered one of the most haunted places in Kent, and across England.
Looking at this this pretty place, you might find it hard to believe
that it was once named the Most Haunted Village in Britain in the Guinness Book of World Records in 1989.
An astounding 12-16 distinctive ghosts have been reported to reside in the village so it’s easy to see why it’s so popular for ghost-hunters hoping to catch a sighting.
Amongst Pluckley’s numerous ghostly tales is that of The Watercress Woman who witnesses
have reported to have seen sitting on Pinnock Bridge smoking her pipe. The elderly woman was well-known
in the village for making a living gathering watercress from the river and selling it to the villagers. It is said that one evening,
after drinking, she fell asleep and her pipe dropped onto her clothing, within moments she was consumed by the flames.
She is believed to still haunt Pinnock Bridge with some witnesses describing a faint pink glow which hovers in the air above the spot where she died.
Many have travelled to Pluckley in the hopes of capturing evidence of spirits,
and one such group asked to spend a night in the village’s Church of St. Nicholas in the 1970s,
bringing along cameras and tape recorders to document their stay. The story goes that the next morning when
the vicar of the church came to greet them, they were disappointed that the night had been uneventful and commented
that the only interesting part had been the Vicar’s dog who had come to visit them a few times, to which the vicar replied that he did not have a dog!
Visit Pluckley to see for yourself why it was given the title of the most haunted village in Britain,
as there are a number of ghost walks and tours held here throughout the year.
Or, visit to appreciate this quintessentially-English village that’s surrounded by beautiful countryside.

Borley Rectory



Borley Rectory, located in the village of Borley in Essex, England, has earned a reputation
as one of the most haunted places in the UK due to a long history of reported paranormal activity.
This reputation is largely attributed to the numerous documented accounts and investigations conducted at the site over the years.
The story of Borley Rectory's haunting began in the late 19th century when the rectory was rebuilt
on the site of an earlier religious building. Reports of strange occurrences soon followed, including ghostly apparitions,
unexplained footsteps, and mysterious lights. The most famous paranormal investigator associated with Borley Rectory
is Harry Price, who conducted extensive investigations in the 1930s. His work and published accounts of the hauntings brought national attention to the rectory.
One of the most famous apparitions reported at Borley Rectory is that of the "Nun."
Witnesses claimed to have seen a spectral figure in a nun's habit wandering the grounds.
Legend has it that this ghost is connected to a tragic love story involving a nun from a nearby convent and a monk from Borley.
Their forbidden love supposedly led to their untimely deaths and eternal unrest.
Other phenomena reported at Borley Rectory included ghostly footsteps, mysterious writing on the walls,
and levitating objects. Additionally, there were claims of poltergeist activity, with objects moving on their own and unexplained noises.
The Borley Rectory was destroyed by fire in 1939, adding a tragic element to its history. Despite the destruction of the building,
reports of paranormal activity continued in the area, further cementing its reputation as a haunted site.
S keptics argue that many of the reported hauntings could be attributed to natural phenomena, hoaxes,
or the power of suggestion. Nevertheless, Borley Rectory's haunted legacy persists, and it remains a subject of fascination
and debate among paranormal enthusiasts and ghost hunters. Its rich history and the extensive documentation of
alleged supernatural events have contributed to its status as one of the most famous and haunted places in the UK.
